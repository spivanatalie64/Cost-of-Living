# Cost-of-Living

THE QUALITY-ADJUSTED SURVIVAL METRIC (QASM)
The true mathematical calculation of working-class survival.

Equation:
QASM = ((W_min * H) - T_inc) + S_net - [ R_base(1 + L_slum) + U(E_leak) + (G_cost + I_deg) + C_core ]

Variables:
* W_min * H: Gross income. (Minimum wage multiplied by hours worked).
* T_inc: State Income Tax. (Washington = 0. Oklahoma = takes a cut of your wages).
* S_net: Total State Subsidies. (Healthcare, food assistance, transit. High in WA, near zero in OK).
* R_base: The base sticker price of rent.
* L_slum: The Slumlord Multiplier. The financial penalty for living in structurally compromised, unregulated buildings with weak tenant protections (out-of-pocket repairs, mold, pests).
* U: Base utility costs.
* E_leak: The Energy Leak Coefficient. The extra cost to heat/cool a poorly insulated box because the state lacks strict energy building codes.
* G_cost: Cost of gas.
* I_deg: The Infrastructure Degradation Penalty. The hidden tax of driving on poorly maintained roads (blown tires, ruined suspensions, extra mechanic visits).
* C_core: Core retail survival goods (baseline groceries, which cost practically the same everywhere).

The Reality:
In states like Washington, a high W_min and massive S_net absorb the higher R_base and G_cost. QASM stays positive. You actually have a life. 

In states like Oklahoma, QASM goes negative. The cheap R_base is completely cannibalized by L_slum, E_leak, and I_deg. You pay for the state's infrastructure and regulation failures out of your own pocket while earning poverty wages.
